# Week 3 — Challenges

1. **[Challenge 1 — Threat-model a real open-source project](challenge-01-threat-model-real-oss-project.md)** — a scoped threat model of a real OSS project of your choice. The on-ramp to the mini-project; do this before starting the mini-project proper. (~3 h)

The challenge differs from the mini-project in scope: the challenge is a focused, time-boxed pass that surfaces *one* high-priority risk and writes a defensible page about it. The mini-project is the full document. Doing the challenge first means you arrive at the mini-project having already done the hardest cognitive work — choosing a project, scoping it, and writing a finding that holds up.

This challenge is a *paper* exercise. You read the source, you reason about attacks, you write a finding. **You do not interact with the live service.** A threat model of a public service is a *desk analysis* of its public artifacts (source code, documentation, deployed binaries you run on your own machine). Running scans or sending crafted payloads against a service you do not own is a crime; it is also not what a threat model is.

Skip the challenge and the mini-project will hurt. Do it and the mini-project will be a straightforward expansion of the same work.
